# Product Guidelines: Diabetactic\n\n## Tone and Voice\n- **Friendly and Encouraging:** The language should be supportive and accessible, tailored to make children and caregivers feel comfortable and capable. While maintaining clinical accuracy, the tone should avoid being overly sterile or intimidating.\n\n## Visual Identity & UI Style\n- **Child-Friendly Modernism:**\n - **Playful yet Professional:** Combine bright, inviting colors and rounded UI elements with the structure and reliability of modern Ionic/Material Design patterns.\n - **Engaging Visuals:** Use clear, friendly icons and potentially simple illustrations to guide users and reduce clinical anxiety.\n - **Reliable Structure:** Maintain standard navigation and layout patterns to ensure the app feels robust and trustworthy for medical use.\n\n## User Experience Principles\n- **Accessibility First:** Ensure high contrast and clear typography to accommodate users of all ages and visual abilities.\n- **Data Clarity:** While the interface is friendly, data presentation (charts, logs) must be precise and easy to read for quick clinical assessment.\n- **Low Friction:** Minimize the steps required to log data or view appointments to encourage consistent use.
